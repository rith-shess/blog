Don't Think I've Forgotten: Cambodia's Lost Rock and Roll




A Khmer vintage song typically features soulful melodies and poetic lyrics that capture themes of love, longing, and nostalgia. Accompanied by traditional instruments like the tro and khim, these songs often reflect the rich cultural heritage of Cambodia. Popular during the golden era of the 1960s and 70s, they are known for their emotional depth and timeless appeal, often performed by legendary singers like Sinn Sisamouth and Ros Sereysothea.

Dance a Go Go and HaLa HaLa


This song is featured on the soundtrack of the documentary "Don't Think I've Forgotten: Cambodia's Lost Rock and Roll," which explores the country's vibrant music scene before the Khmer Rouge era. For those interested in listening to "Dance A Go Go," it is available on various music streaming platforms, including Spotify.

Sinn Sisamouth As A legendary of Music industry in 80s and 90s

Sisamouth’s music was a fusion of genres, blending traditional Cambodian folk tunes with elements of rock and roll, cha-cha, and bossa nova. His ability to modernize Khmer music while keeping its cultural essence intact made his work revolutionary. Sisamouth’s contributions to music have made him an enduring figure in Cambodian culture. He is remembered not only for his voice and lyrics but also as a symbol of resilience and cultural identity.
